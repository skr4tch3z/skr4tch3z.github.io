Some Date Rape Facts to Consider:


  1. Date rape is a felony offense. A conviction will result in a prison sentence and a permanent criminal record.
  2. About two-thirds of American women will experience sexual aggression or rape within their lifetime.
  3. 80% of high school girls will experience some kind of sexual aggression while dating.
  4. Women ages 16-19 are the most prevalent category of date rape.
  5. 10% of date rape victims are males ages 16-24.
  6. 6. The first thing to tell a victim of date rape is: THIS IS NOT YOUR FAULT.


Some Tips to Help Prevent Date Rape


  1. Plan ahead by being informed so that you protect yourself in advance.
  2. Only date guys you REALLY know and trust.
  3. Only go on group or double dates. Surround yourself with trusted friends.
  4. Trust your intuition. If something feels wrong, it probably is wrong.
  5. Be informed. Know who you’re dating. Know his reputation. Get references.
  6. Listen to your friends. Take warnings seriously.
  7. Pay attention to your environment. Where is he taking you? Why?
  8. Don’t drink and date. The most common date rape scenario involves intoxicants.
  9. Know that if you’ve been a victim one time, it increases your chances of being victimized again.
  10. Know that NO means NO. Feel free to scream it loudly if needed. The more noise you can make, the less chance you will become a victim.
  11. Have your phone ready to dial 911 if needed. And don’t be afraid to use it.


For Victims of Date Rape


You have a right to:


  1. Confidentiality and privacy protection upon reporting
  2. Medical care
  3. Accurate information concerning HIV/AIDS and STDs
  4. Counseling
  5. Legal representation and justice
  6. Information about the criminal case during the investigation, trial and corrections system
  7. Information about the offender’s HIV/AIDS status
  8. Compensation and restitution from the offender
